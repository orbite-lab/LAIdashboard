## TactBio view per platform

### Atrigel

Platform value has shifted from IP-protected technology to manufacturing and regulatory moat. Still commercially important via Eligard and Sublocade but low investability as a standalone platform story. Reference point for what happens to in-situ depot platforms after core IP expires.

### BEPO

Market correctly identifies BEPO as the best-positioned non-microsphere in-situ depot platform, but overprices the royalty-compounder transition before olanzapine PDUFA resolves. Contrarian read: Teva concentration risk is material — 100% of near-term royalty revenue derives from a single counterparty — and the AbbVie platform validation is priced in before a single clinical candidate files IND. Kill condition: olanzapine CRL, or Teva reducing commercial investment in UZEDY.

### Biochronomer

Specialty short-duration depot platform with two approved products in two distinct therapeutic areas. Limited platform-licensing optionality given Heron's internal commercialization strategy and short-duration mechanism profile. Monitor Zynrelef commercial trajectory as a leading indicator for postsurgical-pain LAI category economics.

### Chroniject

Capacity provider rather than investable platform. Strategic relevance is primarily as a window into the broader LAI development pipeline — Oakwood's partner roster (when disclosed) reveals which biotechs are pursuing microsphere reformulations. Not a primary investment target on its own merits.

### DepoFoam

Niche local-administration liposomal depot with limited transferability beyond regional anesthesia. Strategic relevance to broader LAI thesis is primarily as a counterexample — demonstrates that not all "depot" platforms generalize across indications. Pacira's investment thesis is product-specific, not platform-driven.

### Durasert

The reference platform for ophthalmic implant LAI. Strategic question is whether EyePoint's pipeline — particularly EYP-1901 in wet AMD — can establish a sustained-release alternative to monthly anti-VEGF injections. If successful, the platform could redefine retinal disease economics; if not, Durasert remains a niche steroid-implant business.

### FluidCrystal

Re-rated platform post-June 2025 Eli Lilly $870M deal. Previously framed as "under-appreciated platform with commercialization-heavy strategy" — the Lilly endorsement converts the dealability narrative. FluidCrystal is now the only LAI platform with both meaningful direct-commercial scale (Buvidal SEK 1.65B / USD 157M FY2024) AND tier-1 pharma external licensing (Lilly cardiometabolic incretins). Watch CAM2029 (Oclaiz) acromegaly resubmission post-CRL and SORENTO NET readout (late 2025/ early 2026) as the next franchise-defining catalysts. Contrarian read: Lilly's mid-single-digit royalty (4-7%) is below what a transformational obesity-LAI platform deal could have commanded — Camurus took meaningful but not extraordinary economics, suggesting Lilly retained leverage on terms despite competitive obesity-LAI dynamics.

### ISM

European challenger platform with single-product commercialization in psych. Strategic question: can Rovi differentiate from BEPO-derived UZEDY or Risperdal Consta on duration, tolerability, or pricing? Without a clear edge, ISM risks becoming the fourth-place risperidone LAI rather than a platform commanding licensing premium.

### Lupron Depot

Tracked as competitive context — Lupron Depot is the price/share anchor for prostate cancer LHRH LAI globally. Any new entrant must position against Lupron's prescriber inertia and Takeda/AbbVie commercial scale. Strategic relevance is in deal pricing — historical Lupron economics inform what new LHRH LAI entrants can realistically command.

### Medisorb

Foundational microsphere platform whose strategic relevance has shifted from technology innovation to manufacturing scale. Alkermes' product portfolio built on Medisorb generates substantial revenue, but the platform itself is no longer a credible licensing story. Reference point for what mature LAI technology looks like post-IP cliff.

### Medusa

French formulation platform with chronic execution challenges. Strategic relevance is primarily as a counterexample for dealability — the 2017 Lilly partnership termination on BioChaperone Lispro illustrates how formulation-platform companies can lose tier-1 relationships when underlying programs disappoint. Tracking value low unless a specific program reaches Phase 3.

### NanoCrystal

Most commercially successful LAI platform by revenue, with Invega franchise and cabotegravir generating multi-billion-dollar combined sales. Strategic value resides in existing product franchises rather than platform licensing potential. Watch Janssen's response as Invega Sustenna faces generic LOE beyond 2030 — Hafyera's 6-monthly duration is the franchise's defensive moat.

### NanoZolid

Speculative early-stage platform with single Phase IIb asset. Strategic interest is twofold — (1) the active-surveillance prostate cancer niche is under-served and could support a niche commercial product; (2) the inorganic-matrix mechanism is genuinely differentiated from polymer depots and may prove more compatible with sensitive payloads. Both theses require Phase IIb data confirmation before becoming actionable.

### Novadur

AbbVie-owned ophthalmic implant platform paralleling Durasert in indication but distinguished by bioerodibility. Strategic interest is comparative — Novadur vs Durasert for a given drug class informs whether reformulation candidates favor erodible (3-6 month) or non-erodible (12-36 month) variants. The recent BEPO-AbbVie deal may also intersect this space as AbbVie's broader LAI strategy evolves.

### Oil decanoate

Tracked for competitive context, not investability. Oil decanoate solutions are the price-anchor floor for LAI markets — any newer platform must justify premium pricing relative to this generic baseline. The continued commercial life of decanoate antipsychotics in cost-sensitive markets is a structural headwind for innovator LAI economics outside high-income geographies.

### PDS (Susvimo)

Not investable directly (Roche-internal) but strategically critical reference for any retinal-disease LAI thesis. PDS effectively defines the upper bound of what biologic LAI can achieve commercially. The recall and relaunch arc is also a cautionary case study for device-formulation hybrid approaches.

### Perseris

Marginal platform tracked for completeness in risperidone LAI competitive set. Indivior's divestiture to Newron suggests the asset is non-core for its original developer; commercial trajectory uncertain. Limited investment thesis on its own.

### PolyActiva

Speculative emerging-platform watchlist entry. PolyActiva represents the next-generation challenger thesis to Durysta in glaucoma — if Phase 3 data supports superior duration with comparable safety, the platform could attract Big Pharma interest. Pre-clinical-readout, pure optionality.

### Probuphine

Tracked as competitive context and as a cautionary tale — Probuphine illustrates that LAI duration alone does not win markets; site-of-care workflow and prescriber preference matter more. The 6-month duration was insufficient compensation for surgical insertion friction. Strategic read-across to any prospective implant LAI in indications where injectable alternatives exist.

### Sandostatin LAR

Reference product for peptide LAI commercial economics in endocrine oncology. Strategic read-across — Camurus CAM2029 (octreotide via FluidCrystal) directly challenges this franchise; Sandostatin LAR's current sales trajectory under generic and biosimilar pressure provides the price ceiling for any new octreotide LAI entrant.

### Signifor LAR

Niche endocrine LAI tracked for competitive context. Strategic relevance is as a precedent for rare-disease LAI economics — Recordati's acquisition from Novartis demonstrates that mature LAI franchises can be transferred between strategics at meaningful multiples even with patent expiry approaching.

### Somatuline Autogel

Reference peptide LAI for somatostatin analog category with novel self-assembly mechanism. Tracked for competitive context against Sandostatin LAR and Camurus CAM2029. Unusual mechanism makes this a technically distinctive entry that warrants ongoing attention even as core IP runs off.

### SpectruM

Asian-domiciled (Taiwan) platform commercializing in Western markets via partner — interesting model but not a platform commanding licensing premium. Camcevi competes with Eligard, Lupron Depot, and Trelstar in mature LHRH category. Investment thesis is product-economics-driven, not platform-driven.

### Trelstar

Tracked for competitive context in LHRH LAI category. Decapeptyl/Trelstar's European market share is durable despite IP expiry — the LHRH LAI category has demonstrated strong post-LOE persistence due to prescriber inertia and manufacturing barriers. Read-across for new entrants: take share will require structural advantage (duration, tolerability, or pricing), not just regulatory approval.

### Zoladex

Reference solid-implant platform for peptide LAI. Tracked for indication-fit context in the platform H2H matrix. Competitive context for any new solid- rod implant entrant — Zoladex's commercial entrenchment in oncology hormonal therapy is durable despite IP expiry.

### iDose

Internal Glaukos asset, not externally investable as platform. Strategic significance for us is as a benchmark — iDose's longer duration pressures Allergan's Durysta and sets a higher bar for any new ophthalmic LAI platform. Watch commercial uptake post-launch as a leading indicator for prescriber/patient acceptance of multi-year ocular implants.
